V - model

November 20, 2020

In the V model development and testing activity is started based on requirement specification document. Developer team work in the design and after that start implementation, testing team plans testing. Activities are happening parallel. Activities in Waterfall model and in V model are characterized as similar (STC Admin, 2012).

V-model can also be called Verification and Validation model. Testing activity happens in each phase. Typical representation of the V model represents software development activities on the left side and testing phases on the right side. The process of the “doing” is followed by developers, and the process of “checking” is followed by testing team. The V model typically consists of Unit testing, Integration testing, System testing and acceptance testing (STC Admin, 2012).

V model (Tutorialspoint, 2020)

Main advantages of the V model are the simple usage of it, it takes less time, as most of the activities, for example, designing and testing takes place before the coding part starts. It is possible to find different defects in the early stage, and it avoids downward flow of the defects. Model is popular because it is simple and straight forward (TestingBrain, 2019).

When turning to the disadvantages of the V shaped model, it is not very flexible. No prototypes are developed and it means there is a less chance of meeting the customer expectation. If there are some problems in the process, whole document, also testing and requirement part has to be updated all over again. V model is also characterized as risky model that takes a lot of time to use (TestingBrain, 2019).
